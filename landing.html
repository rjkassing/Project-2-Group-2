Data Sources

The primary dataset for this project is a survey of more than 20,000 people who started coding within the last five years, distributed by Free Code Camp. Free Code Camp is an open source community teachning people to code and build projects for nonprofits. Surveyors reached out to participants through Twitter and email lists of various organizations that help people learn to code (like Georgia Tech Bootcamps!). The goal of the survey was to understand people's motivations for learning to code, how they're learning to code, their demographics, and their socioeconomic background.

The secondary dataset for this project is an Employee Diversity in Tech dataset that summarizes global employee data from several leading technology firms where data were publicly available. These data are not exhaustive; not all companies declare their figures, and most donâ€™t reveal the ratio of manual labourers to executives. Data were collected on a yearly basis between 2014-2018. Diversity is measured based on race of employees in the following categories: White, Asian, Latino, Black, Multi, Other, and Undeclared. Gender categories were Male and Female. More information is available at informationisbeautiful.net where we accessed the latest dataset.


Summary of Findings

These data show that:

  • 67% of participants live outside the United States
  • The median age of participants is about 30 years old
  • 21% of particiants identify as women
  • About a quarter of participants identify as being part of an ethnic minority in their country
  • 8% of participants are military veterans
  • Particpants have been coding for an average of 21 months
  • 17% of participants already landed their first developer job
